\begin{solution}{exo:g7:literal:2}
$5ab = 5 \times a \times b$; \quad
$3(x+2) = 3 \times (x + 2)$; \quad
$x^2 + 4x = x \times x + 4 \times x$; \quad
$2a^3 = 2 \times a \times a \times a$.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g7:literal:8}
$3(x+4) = 3x + 12$: \emph{true} for every $x$ (distributivity ---
this is a proof, no test needed).

$2x + 5 = 7x$: test $x = 2$: left $9$, right $14$ --- \emph{false} in
general (it holds only for $x = 1$).

$x + x = x^2$: test $x = 3$: left $6$, right $9$ --- \emph{false} in
general ($x + x = 2x$).
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g7:literal:9}
\emph{1.} Length: $w + 3$. Perimeter:
\[
P = 2\bigl(w + (w + 3)\bigr) = 2(2w + 3) = 4w + 6 .
\]

\emph{2.} For $w = 5$: directly, the sides are $5$ and $8$, so
$P = 2 \times 13 = 26$ cm; with the formula, $4 \times 5 + 6 = 26$ cm.
They agree.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g7:literal:10}
Following the steps with the letter $n$:
\[
n \ \to\ n + 6 \ \to\ 3(n + 6) = 3n + 18 \ \to\ 3n + 18 - 18 = 3n
\ \to\ \frac{3n}{n} = 3 .
\]
Everyone gets $3$.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g7:literal:11}
As a difference: $A = a^2 - b^2$. By cutting the L into two
rectangles: a full-width strip of height $a - b$ and, on top of it, a
narrower rectangle:
\[
A = a(a - b) + b(a - b)
\]
(bottom rectangle $a$ wide and $a - b$ tall, top rectangle $a - b$
wide and $b$ tall gives the variant $a(a-b) + (a-b)b$ --- the same).
For $a = 5$, $b = 2$: $a^2 - b^2 = 25 - 4 = 21$, and
$5 \times 3 + 2 \times 3 = 15 + 6 = 21$. Both expressions agree ---
they always do: this is the identity $a^2 - b^2 = (a + b)(a - b)$ of
\cref{ch:g9:algebra} in disguise.
\end{solution}
