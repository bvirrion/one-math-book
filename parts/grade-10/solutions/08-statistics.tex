\section*{Chapter \ref{ch:g10:stats} --- Descriptive Statistics}

\begin{solution}{exo:g10:stats:1}
Sum: $0+1+1+2+2+2+3+3+4+4+5+6+7+8+12 = 60$, so the mean is
$\frac{60}{15} = 4$. The series is already sorted and $N = 15$ is odd:
the median is the $8$th value, $3$. Range: $12 - 0 = 12$.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g10:stats:2}
Frequencies: $\frac{7}{50} = 0.14$, $\frac{9}{50} = 0.18$,
$\frac{8}{50} = 0.16$, $\frac{10}{50} = 0.20$, $\frac{6}{50} = 0.12$,
$\frac{10}{50} = 0.20$ (sum $1$). Mean outcome:
\[
\bar x = \frac{7 \times 1 + 9 \times 2 + 8 \times 3 + 10 \times 4
+ 6 \times 5 + 10 \times 6}{50}
= \frac{7 + 18 + 24 + 40 + 30 + 60}{50}
= \frac{179}{50} = 3.58 .
\]
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g10:stats:3}
$N = 12$ (even): the median is the midpoint of the $6$th and $7$th
values, both equal to $6$: median $6$.

$Q_1$: $\frac{12}{4} = 3$, so $Q_1$ is the $3$rd value: $4$.

$Q_3$: $\frac{3 \times 12}{4} = 9$, so $Q_3$ is the $9$th value: $8$.

Box plot: box from $4$ to $8$ with the median bar at $6$, whiskers from
$1$ to $11$.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g10:stats:4}
The $24$ scores total $24 \times 11.5 = 276$. With the new score the
total is $276 + 19 = 295$ for $25$ students: new mean
$\frac{295}{25} = 11.8$.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g10:stats:5}
Current total: $5 \times 12 = 60$. A mean of $13$ over $6$ tests requires
a total of $78$, so the $6$th score must be $78 - 60 = 18$. A mean of
$14$ would require $84 - 60 = 24 > 20$: not reachable.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g10:stats:6}
Mean larger than $20$: for instance
$1,\ 2,\ 3,\ 10,\ 40,\ 50,\ 60$ --- median $10$ (the $4$th sorted value),
mean $\frac{166}{7} \approx 23.7$.

Mean smaller than $6$: for instance
$0,\ 0,\ 0,\ 10,\ 10,\ 10,\ 10$ --- median $10$, mean
$\frac{40}{7} \approx 5.7$.

The mean cannot go below $\frac{40}{7}$: for the median (the $4$th sorted
value) to be $10$, the values in positions $4$ to $7$ must all be
$\geq 10$, so the total is at least $40$ and the mean at least
$\frac{40}{7}$ --- the second example is extremal.

These examples show that the mean and the median are largely independent:
knowing one says little about the other, which is why a good summary
reports both.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g10:stats:7}
\emph{1.} Mean:
$\frac{9 \times 1800 + 12000}{10} = \frac{16200 + 12000}{10} = 2820$.
Median: sorted, the $10$ salaries are nine values $1800$ then $12000$;
the median is the midpoint of the $5$th and $6$th values, both $1800$:
median $1800$.

\emph{2.} The median ($1800$) describes the typical salary: it is what
$9$ employees out of $10$ actually earn. The mean ($2820$) is pulled up
by the single high salary and matches nobody's payslip.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g10:stats:8}
Total height: $12 \times 168 + 18 \times 163 = 2016 + 2934 = 4950$ cm for
$30$ students, so the mean is $\frac{4950}{30} = 165$ cm. It is closer to
the girls' mean because they are more numerous (weighted mean, weights
$12$ and $18$).
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g10:stats:9}
\emph{1.} The new mean is
\[
\bar y = \frac{y_1 + \dots + y_N}{N}
= \frac{(a x_1 + b) + \dots + (a x_N + b)}{N}
= \frac{a(x_1 + \dots + x_N) + Nb}{N}
= a \bar x + b .
\]

\emph{2.} With $a = 1.8$ and $b = 32$: mean
$1.8 \times 20 + 32 = 68$ degrees Fahrenheit.
\end{solution}
