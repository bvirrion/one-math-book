\begin{solution}{exo:g5:decimals:2}
The $4$ counts the tenths, the $8$ the thousandths:
\[
27.418 = 27 + \frac{4}{10} + \frac{1}{100} + \frac{8}{1000}.
\]
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:decimals:3}
$3.60 = 3.6$: true (a final \omterm{def:g1:counting:zero}{zero} adds nothing).

$0.5 = 0.05$: false --- $5$ tenths against $5$ hundredths.

$2.070 = 2.07$: true.

$1.3 = 1.03$: false --- $3$ tenths against $3$ hundredths.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:decimals:10}
Compare the tenths first: $7.12$ has $1$ tenth, $7.9$ has $9$
tenths, so $7.12 < 7.9$. Lucie compared the decimal parts as whole
numbers ($12$ vs $9$), forgetting that $12$ hundredths is far less
than $90$ hundredths.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:decimals:11}
\omterm{def:g4:numbers:round}{Rounding} to the tenth gives $6.4$ for the two-decimal numbers from
$6.35$ (halfway \omterm{def:g4:numbers:round}{rounds} up) to $6.44$: the numbers $6.35$, $6.36$,
\dots, $6.44$. Smallest: $6.35$; biggest: $6.44$.
\end{solution}
