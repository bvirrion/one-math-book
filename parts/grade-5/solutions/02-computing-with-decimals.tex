\begin{solution}{exo:g5:decimalops:6}
$6.2 \times 4$: estimate $24$; exactly $24.8$.

$3.45 \times 8$: estimate $28$ ($3.5 \times 8$); exactly $27.6$.

$12.5 \times 6$: estimate $75$; exactly $75$.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:decimalops:7}
$7$ laps: $0.4 \times 7 = 2.8$ km. $25$ laps:
$0.4 \times 25 = 10$ km.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:decimalops:8}
Baguettes: $1.15 \times 2 = 2.30$. Total: $2.30 + 12.60 = 14.90$.
Change: $20 - 14.90 = 5.10$.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:decimalops:9}
Pack: $1.5 \times 6 = 9$ L. Days: $0.75 + 0.75 = 1.5$ L every two
days, so $9$ L last $12$ days (twelve servings of $0.75$:
$12 \times 0.75 = 9$).
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:decimalops:10}
Estimate: $5.6$ is more than $5.5$, and $5.5 \times 3 = 16.5$ --- so
the product must be \emph{more} than $16.5$, and $15.18$ is too
small. Correctly: $56 \times 3 = 168$, one decimal digit, so
$5.6 \times 3 = 16.8$. Milo glued his two partial products ($15$ and
$18$) side by side instead of adding them with their places:
$15 + 1.8 = 16.8$.
\end{solution}
