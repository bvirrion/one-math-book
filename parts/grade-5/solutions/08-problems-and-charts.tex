\section*{Chapter \ref{ch:g5:data} --- Problems and Charts}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:data:1}
Tickets: $32 \times 7 = 224$. Total: $240 + 224 = 464$.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:data:2}
Per one kg: $7.50 \div 3 = 2.50$. Five kg:
$5 \times 2.50 = 12.50$.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:data:3}
$87 \div 6 = 14.5$: each pays $14.50$.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:data:4}
Spent: $13.90 + 8.50 = 22.40$. Left: $25 - 22.40 = 2.60$.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:data:5}
More than $50$ mm: January ($60$), March ($55$), April ($70$), May
($80$). May minus June: $80 - 30 = 50$ mm.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:data:6}
Week $2$: $6$ cm. Growth of $4$ cm: between weeks $2$ and $3$
($6 \to 10$), and between weeks $4$ and $5$ ($15 \to 19$).
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:data:7}
Four bars of heights $4$, $7$, $3$, $6$ (graduation every $1$ or
$2$ goals). Total: $4 + 7 + 3 + 6 = 20$ goals.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:data:8}
Pack per one: $3.20 \div 8 = 0.40$. Saving per yogurt:
$0.50 - 0.40 = 0.10$ (ten cents).
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:data:9}
Way 1: Saturday $148 \times 9.50 = 1\,406$; Sunday
$95 \times 9.50 = 902.50$; difference $503.50$.

Way 2: $148 - 95 = 53$ more tickets, each worth $9.50$:
$53 \times 9.50 = 503.50$. Same answer --- the difference of the
takings is the taking of the difference.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:data:10}
Per person: $300 \div 4 = 75$ g. For ten:
$10 \times 75 = 750$ g of rice.
\end{solution}

\begin{solution}{exo:g5:data:11}
Many correct problems --- for instance: ``Milo buys $3$ juices at
$1.20$ each and a sandwich at $3.40$. He pays with a $12.50$
voucher. How much of the voucher is left?'' Solution: juices
$3 \times 1.20 = 3.60$; total spent $3.60 + 3.40 = 7$; left
$12.50 - 7 = 5.50$.
\end{solution}
