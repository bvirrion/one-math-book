\chapter{Preface}

This book is an open-source project with a simple ambition: a single,
coherent mathematics book covering everything from kindergarten to the end
of the bachelor's degree, written in English for readers anywhere in the
world.

The style is concise and rigorous: courses built from definitions, examples,
propositions and theorems, with proofs whenever they are accessible at the
given level, followed by carefully chosen exercises. Results stated without
proof are explicitly marked as admitted. Full solutions to all exercises are
collected at the end of the book.

Each school year is a part of the book, and each part is split into
chapters. The current state of the project covers Grade 12 (age 17--18),
the final year of secondary school, at the level of an advanced mathematics
track.

\section*{How to read this book}

Statements are numbered within each chapter and color-coded: definitions in
blue, theorems in red, propositions and their relatives in orange, and
methods --- short recipes for standard tasks --- in green. Exercises are
graded from ($\star$) for direct applications to ($\star\star\star$) for
harder problems.
